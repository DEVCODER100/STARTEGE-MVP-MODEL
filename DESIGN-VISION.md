# Stratège Design Vision

## North star

Stratège is not “an AI chatbot for marketing.”

It is a personal strategy desk for founders: a place where business moments become clear marketing decisions and finished creative work.

## Desired feeling

Users should feel:

- “It already understands my business.”
- “I can see why it recommended this.”
- “The work is nearly finished.”
- “This feels thoughtful, not generated.”
- “I am directing a strategist, not prompting a machine.”

## Product metaphor

The interface combines:

- an editorial strategy notebook;
- a creative director’s markup;
- a calm production workspace.

Chat is the control layer. The artifact canvas is the product.

## Signature visual language

### Strategy annotations

Use numbered decisions, margin notes, underlines, connection rules, and “why this” callouts to expose the reasoning behind an output.

### Paper and canvas

Use warm paper surfaces and crisp white artifacts. Grids appear only where work is being composed. Avoid a universal decorative dot background.

### Controlled asymmetry

Prefer editorial layouts with unequal columns, cropped artifacts, generous margins, and deliberate alignment over centered stacks of equal cards.

### Human accent

Keep strategy green, but introduce a restrained warm orange/coral for milestones, human moments, and publishing states.

## Core workspace

Desktop:

`Projects rail | Conversation | Artifact canvas`

Mobile:

`Talk | Work`

Every major workflow creates an artifact:

- campaign plan;
- daily post;
- visual;
- ad;
- content review;
- weekly strategy.

Artifacts are modular, directly editable, versioned, and exportable.

## Interaction principles

1. Show the user’s brand context when it affects an answer.
2. Explain important recommendations in one sentence.
3. Regenerate blocks, not entire outputs.
4. Prefer visible choices over asking users to invent prompts.
5. Transform conversation into structured work as early as possible.
6. Make the next useful action obvious.
7. Let the user override the AI without fighting the interface.

## Motion principles

Motion represents:

- input becoming insight;
- insight becoming a draft;
- a draft becoming a publishable artifact.

No ornamental AI spectacle. No endless floating objects. Motion must clarify state, hierarchy, or causality.

## Anti-generic rules

Do not use:

- purple/blue AI gradients;
- floating glass cards;
- sparkle icons as the main motif;
- feature-card grids with identical boxes;
- a centered chatbot as the primary product screen;
- fake testimonials or fake metrics;
- vague copy such as “10x your creativity”;
- animation on every viewport entrance.

## Quality bar

The final design should feel ownable even in grayscale. Color and animation should strengthen an already distinctive composition, not rescue a generic one.
