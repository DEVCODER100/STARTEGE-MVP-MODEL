Founder Story

A year ago I built my first real product. Velora — an AI wardrobe app.

I spent months on it. Built feature after feature. Auth, wardrobe management, outfit generation, AI stylist, ratings, credits, payments. Everything I thought a real product should have.

Then I launched it.

Nothing happened.

No users. No traction. No clue why.

The truth was harder to accept — the idea wasn't strong enough. And I had no idea how to market it. I had built 12 features before talking to a single user.

That's when it hit me — this isn't just my problem. Every technical founder I know has this exact struggle. We're great at building. We love building. But marketing? Writing hooks? Knowing what to post? That's where most of us break.

The product gets built. The distribution never happens.

So I decided to start over. For weeks I lived inside founder communities — Discord, Reddit, Twitter, LinkedIn. I didn't pitch anything. I just listened. I asked founders what their hardest part was. Not building. Not funding. Marketing.

I heard the same sentence over and over:

"I build every day, but I don't know how to turn that into content."

That was the insight. The pain wasn't lack of AI tools. It was the mental tax of translating work into words.

So I built the first version of Stratège around that exact problem. Showed founders. Got brutal feedback. Threw away half. Iterated. Showed again. Threw away more.

Today the MVP is live.

Tell it what you shipped today → get 3 post angles in your own voice. Not generic AI writing. Your voice.

This is what I wish I had when I was building Velora.
