# Prompt for Lovable, Replit, v0, Claude, or another design builder

Copy the prompt below and attach:

- desktop and mobile screenshots of the current landing page;
- screenshots of dashboard, onboarding, brand profile, generated image flow, pricing, signup, and login;
- examples of real generated outputs;
- the current logo and brand assets.

---

You are redesigning **Stratège**, an AI marketing product for founders and small businesses.

Do not make a generic AI SaaS website. Do not use purple-blue gradients, glowing blobs, floating glass cards, sparkle-heavy decoration, or a ChatGPT clone. The result must feel art-directed, credible, editorial, and specific to this product.

## Product truth

Stratège learns a founder’s business, audience, city, tone, goals, platforms, and visual identity. A founder can describe a business moment or marketing need in ordinary language. Stratège turns it into strategic recommendations and finished artifacts such as posts, hooks, captions, visuals, ads, campaign plans, and WhatsApp copy.

The key experience is not “talking to AI.” It is watching raw business context become clear marketing work.

## Creative direction: The Strategy Desk

Design Stratège as a personal strategist’s working desk:

- editorial strategy notebook;
- creative-director annotations;
- calm production canvas;
- visible reasoning;
- polished, directly editable artifacts.

Chat is a compact control layer. The artifact canvas is the visual hero.

Create a recognizable signature system using:

- numbered strategic decisions;
- fine annotation lines;
- margin notes;
- underlined key phrases;
- small “why this works” callouts;
- connections showing which brand detail influenced an output;
- warm paper surfaces and crisp white artifacts;
- controlled asymmetry and varied editorial scale.

## Visual system

Suggested colors:

- Ink `#171713`
- Paper `#F5F1E8`
- Canvas `#FBFAF6`
- Strategy green `#087A55`
- Deep green `#064B39`
- Human accent `#E8793B`
- Rule `#D8D2C5`
- Muted text `#706D65`

Use a characterful editorial serif such as Fraunces selectively for emotional or strategic phrases. Use a neutral sans for UI and body text. A compact mono may be used only for metadata.

Do not put every section in a rounded card. Use columns, rules, whitespace, crop, overlap, annotations, and different surface levels. Rounded corners should be restrained and consistent.

## Landing page

Create an asymmetrical hero.

Left side:

- headline: “Your business already has stories. Stratège turns them into marketing.”
- supporting copy: “Tell Stratège what happened today. Leave with the post, visual, campaign angle, and next move—shaped around your actual brand.”
- primary CTA: “Create my first post”
- secondary text link: “Watch it work”
- private-beta reassurance.

Right side:

Create a believable interactive transformation:

1. Founder enters: “We packed our first 100 orders today.”
2. Stratège extracts three possible story angles.
3. “Behind the scenes” is selected.
4. A reel script, caption, visual direction, and posting time assemble into an artifact.
5. Show: “Recommended because this brand’s audience responds to founder-led stories.”

The animation should take 8–15 seconds, loop calmly, and have a reduced-motion version.

Landing page section order:

1. Interactive transformation hero.
2. “One sentence → one week of marketing” horizontal narrative.
3. Real output gallery showing input, strategic choice, final output, and outcome.
4. Brand-memory explanation with visible editable context.
5. Three workflow stories: daily post, launch campaign, paid ad.
6. Honest beta/customer proof.
7. Complete pricing.
8. Final CTA.

Do not create a generic equal-card feature grid.

## Main app

Desktop layout:

- narrow project/recent-work rail;
- compact conversation column;
- large artifact canvas.

Mobile layout:

- two primary views: “Talk” and “Work”;
- preserve draft state when switching.

Replace abstract AI modes such as Coach/Strategy/Create with user jobs:

- Plan a campaign
- Make today’s post
- Create an ad
- Review my content
- Find my next move

Each job opens a distinct artifact template.

Assistant responses should be structured into editable blocks:

- Recommendation
- Why this fits your brand
- Draft
- Visual direction
- Next action
- Alternatives

Add a collapsible “Using from your brand” strip showing audience, tone, goal, city, platform, product photo, and colors. Every item can be removed or overridden for the current request.

## Generated artifact

Design a professional content canvas with:

- title, status, and platform;
- social preview;
- editable hook, caption, CTA, hashtags, and visual;
- a margin note explaining the strategic choice;
- regenerate-this-block controls;
- version history;
- one publishing/export bar;
- posted/result feedback.

Do not leave generated images as small chat attachments.

## Image creative direction flow

Present three genuinely different visual directions on a creative board. Each direction shows:

- composition;
- palette;
- typography mood;
- sample crop;
- explanation of why it fits the brand.

Allow mixing a palette from one direction with a composition from another. Show image credit cost before generating.

## Onboarding

Reduce the feeling of twelve separate questions.

Flow:

1. Import website or Instagram.
2. Show extracted brand profile on one review screen.
3. Ask only missing or uncertain information.
4. Organize remaining inputs into Business, Audience & Goals, and Voice & Visual Identity.
5. Generate a useful first post before onboarding ends.

Use chapter progress, not twelve tiny dots.

## Brand profile

Design this as a living brand book:

- brand summary;
- customer profiles;
- voice traits and example phrases;
- words to use and avoid;
- visual palette and type;
- content pillars;
- product media;
- platform preferences;
- recent things Stratège learned, requiring approval.

Allow inline editing per section. Do not force users to repeat onboarding.

## Pricing and auth

Create a complete pricing page. Prefer a free/private-beta plan, one clear Pro plan, and optional image-credit packs if that matches the business model. Explain exactly what consumes credits.

Signup should promise an immediate outcome: “Create your brand profile and leave with your first ready-to-post idea.”

Desktop auth can use a split layout with the form plus a real rotating output/customer story. Keep login simpler.

## Motion

Use motion only to explain transformation and state:

- request becomes artifact;
- recommendation expands into a canvas;
- selected brand details connect to the output they influenced;
- blocks assemble in sequence;
- direct-manipulation controls use subtle spring motion.

Timing:

- micro-interactions 120–180 ms;
- panels 240–360 ms;
- support `prefers-reduced-motion`.

Avoid parallax overload, floating blobs, animated gradients, sparkle particles, and identical fade-up animation on every section.

## UX requirements

- Responsive from 360 px mobile through large desktop.
- WCAG-aware contrast and keyboard focus.
- 44 px minimum touch targets on mobile.
- Auto-save drafts with visible saved state.
- Undo for destructive actions.
- Skeletons preserve final layout.
- Clear errors with recovery actions.
- Selected states do not rely on color alone.
- No fake testimonials, fake logos, or invented metrics.

## Deliverables

Create:

1. high-fidelity desktop and mobile landing page;
2. dashboard empty state;
3. active conversation plus artifact canvas;
4. generated post/ad artifact;
5. image creative-direction chooser;
6. onboarding import and profile-review screens;
7. living brand profile;
8. pricing;
9. signup and login;
10. a design-token and component specification;
11. motion notes for every important transition.

Use real-looking content for an Indian founder or small business, including Instagram and WhatsApp use cases, without stereotypes.

Before finalizing each screen, ask: “Could this belong to another AI startup if the logo changed?” If yes, redesign it until the Strategy Desk metaphor and visible brand reasoning make it unmistakably Stratège.
